Zero Hunger

Collab with GHWB

This collaborative effort between the Zero Hunger initiative and Good Health & Well-Being (GHWB) addresses the deep connection between food security and public health. Poor nutrition is a leading cause of health problems worldwide, and this project works to ensure that vulnerable populations receive both adequate food and medical support.

Key Features:

  • Nutritious Meal Programs: Focuses on distributing balanced meals that meet dietary needs, especially for children, pregnant women, and the elderly.
  • Medical and Nutritional Screenings: Offers free health check-ups to identify malnutrition, deficiencies, and food-related illnesses.
  • Hygiene & Sanitation Campaigns: Provides clean drinking water, hygiene kits, and education on food safety practices.
  • Policy Advocacy: Works with governments and organizations to push for food security policies and healthcare access for underprivileged communities.

Objectives:

  • Nutritious Meal Distribution: Deliver well-balanced meals rich in vitamins and minerals to vulnerable populations.
  • Medical Check-Ups: Offer free health screenings for conditions related to malnutrition, including anemia and vitamin deficiencies.
  • Clean Water and Hygiene: Implement water purification systems and hygiene programs to prevent foodborne illnesses.
  • Advocacy for Policy Changes: Work with governments to improve food security policies and healthcare accessibility.

Impact:

Over 100,000 individuals have received nutritional support, and more than 500 communities have benefited from improved sanitation and food safety programs.
